Research

Research Interests

Identifying Unidentified PeVatron Sources

Characterizing ultra-high-energy gamma-ray sources beyond the PeV scale using multi-wavelength and multi-messenger observations with Fermi-LAT, HAWC, and IceCube.

Neutrino Astrophysics

Analyzing potential neutrino emitters from within and beyond the galaxy to constrain the hadronic fraction of the observed gamma rays.

Machine Learning for Event Reconstruction

Developing scalable reconstruction and classification algorithms for IceCube neutrino track and shower events, increasing observatory sensitivity.

Image Processing in Astrophysics

Exploring computer vision techniques for source detection and seeding algorithms applied to gamma-ray sky maps.

About

Hello, I'm
Rishi Babu

I'm Rishi and I am currently a postdoctoral resaercher at Michigan State University working on the HAWC gamma-ray observatory and the IceCube Neutrino Observatory. I currently serve as the Data Analysis Coordinator for the HAWC Collaboration, providing scientific leadership and coordinating analysis efforts across the collaboration. I also serve as the lead for the Public Data Release for HAWC, overseeing the development and release of high-level data products to the public.

My research focuses on multi-messenger astrophysics, particularly studying high-energy phenomena in our galaxy through gamma-ray and neutrino observations. I develop advanced data analysis softwares, including machine learning algorithms, to identify and characterize cosmic accelerators from large scale surveys for the HAWC collaboration. I also work on IceCube Collaboration, contributing to the developement of advanced neutrino source detection techniques using image processing techniques. I'm also the lead on the neutrino track reconstruction group using machine learning algorithms providing scalable and efficient techniques using JAX. I am mentoring several undergraduate and graduate students in their research projects, and am passionate about mentoring early-career researchers and promoting diversity and inclusion in STEM fields.

I received my Ph.D and Master's degrees from Michigan Technological University, where I was advised by Petra Huentemeyer, spokesperson of the HAWC Collaboration and the SWGO Collaboration. My PhD research focused on identifying PeVatron accelerators in our galaxy using gamma-ray observations from HAWC. I developed the charge calibration software for the HAWC outrigger array which was deployed in 2019. I also serve as one of the 10 experts on observatory control, managing the data acquisition for the HAWC experiment.

I have a Bachelors degree in Engineering Physics from the National Institute of Technology, Calicut, India. During my during my undergraduate studies, I have worked on several different projects ranging from involvement in development of robotics for the Asia competition Robocon, and development and studies of micro-magnetic semiconductor materials. I had an inkling for research and wanted to pursue a career in scientific research, which led me to apply for graduate school in the US.

Apart from research, I am passionate about travel and exploring new cultures. I have been fortunate to travel to different countries and it has a become a major part of my life after I met my wonderful wife Adarsha. If you dont see me posting pictures about travels, I am mostly in the kitchen experimenting with new recipes and cuisines.

Fun and a rarely known fact about me: I was part of the Michigan Tech Varisty Esports Team where I competed in collegiate Rainbow Six Siege tournaments. I am a huge fan of video games that drive creativity and story telling.

For outreach, I have given several public talks and workshops at Michigan State University including a public seminar at the MSU Planetarium, and have been a regular participant in the Michigan State Science Festival where I represent the High Energy Physics group to talk about high-energy particles from space, with a live cloud chamber demo of cosmic ray detection. I have also been involved in mentoring high school students through the IceCube Masterclass program, where I provide hands-on experience with particle detection in a laboratory setting.

I am also a proud mentor and advocate for diversity and inclusion in STEM fields. I try to actively being involved in initiatives to promote equity, diversity, and inclusion in academia and share my experiences. During my PhD, I had observed that the retention of underrepresented and minority students in the physics department is a significant challenge. Therefore, I made a decision to commit myself to mentoring and supporting students from underrepresented backgrounds in physics so that they can have a more positive and inclusive experience in academia.
