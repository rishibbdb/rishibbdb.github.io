Research

Research Interests

Identifying unidentified PeVatron Sources

Characterizing ultra-high-energy gamma-ray sources, beyong the PeV scale, using multi-wavelength and multi-messenger observations particularly with Fermi-LAT, HAWC and IceCube observatories.

Neutrino Astrophysics

Analyzing potential neutrino emitters from within the galaxy and also outside the galaxy to constrain the hadronic fraction of the observed gamma rays.

Machine learning applications to data reconstructions

Developing scalable and computationally efficient reconstruction and classification algorithms for IceCube neutrino track and shower events, increasing the sensitivity of the observatory.

Image processing applications to Astrophysics

Exploring the use of image processing techniques to enhance the analysis of astronomical data, particularly in the context of source seeding and detection algorithms for deep learning and computer vision.

Public Engagement

Outreach

Bringing the universe closer to general public so that they can appreciate the wonders of astrophysics, even if a tiny bit — through public talks and educational initiatives.

Michigan State Science Festival

Representing the High Energy Physics group at the Michigan State Science Festival to talk about the high energy particles coming from space and give a demo of how we can detect these astronomical particles on Earth using a cloud chamber.

IceCube Masterclass

Participating in the IceCube Masterclass to educate high school students about neutrino astrophysics at the South Pole using IceCube Observatory and provide hands-on experience of obsering particles in-lab.

Public Seminar at the MSU Planetarium

Gave a public seminar at the MSU Planetarium to create awareness among the general public that astrophysics is more than just observing the universe through a telescope with visible light. Educated them on the evolution and the death of stars and the high-energy observations of what remains after.
